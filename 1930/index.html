Now that Material Theme turned into an LLC, what’s next?

May 10, 2021 by degecko

This is going to come off as a rant, although I’m not really mad, I’m just very curios how other users see this situation.

For those who don’t know, Material Theme is a popular PhpStorm (and other editors) theme. Yes, I said theme, otherwise known as style, skin or template. It features a couple subtle syntax highlighting themes, both dark and light mode, as well as IDE interface themes to match them.

I get that the creator spent a lot of time on it, to make it a pretty complex plugin, as I see it updating at least as often as PhpStorm itself. I find it pleasant, otherwise I’d drop it. I also don’t have a problem paying 17 euros a year, after taxes, to use a theme.

However, what I do have a problem with is turning a profit on something that used to be free, based on flawed logic. You have an audience, they like your stuff, so what do you do? You turn some features into “advanced” features. Not only that, but those features apply mostly only to Mac users, which you know that are used to pay for software. I’m referring now to the custom titlebar feature, which changes the background color of the title bar. I have to pay 17 euros a year to get that. Really?! They couldn’t come up with a solid new feature to actually deserve the premium tier.

Anyway, what do you guys think about this? I’m ready to admit to being toxic for nothing, and believe me, I know perfectly well that I’m complaining about a theme, but seriously, I don’t know what to make of this. I’ve never seen an IDE theme turn into an LLC before.
